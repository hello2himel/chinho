Sources & Confessions

Every observation came from your own browser, in the first milliseconds after you arrived. A few honest footnotes follow.
  • Your location
    ipapi.co · Free tier · CORS-enabled
    Your IP address arrives in the header of every request your device makes. We pass it to ipapi.co to translate it into a city and ISP name. Neither side stores it. We display only the first and last octet on screen.
  • Browser APIs
    MDN Web Docs · Mozilla · CC-BY-SA 2.5
    Every observation about your device was retrieved through standard JavaScript APIs. No exploits. No hacks. Everything on this page is by design. The design is the problem.
  • Font fingerprinting
    EFF · Cover Your Tracks
    Detecting installed fonts by measuring rendered text widths has been documented since 2010. Most browsers are unique enough to be tracked across the open web without any cookie at all.
  • Canvas fingerprinting
    Princeton University · Web Transparency & Accountability Project
    A 2014 study found canvas fingerprinting on 5% of the top 100,000 websites. Your browser supports the technique. We did not use it. The page you visit next might.
  • Battery research
    Olejnik, Englehardt, Narayanan · 2015 · "The Leaking Battery"
    Battery percentage combined with discharge time was shown to uniquely track users across websites for up to thirty minutes — without cookies, without accounts. Firefox removed this API in 2016. Chrome and Edge still expose it.
  • What this page sent
    Nothing to third parties
    No external analytics, no advertising beacons, no tracking pixels. The IP geolocation lookup uses a CORS‑friendly service that retains nothing. All fingerprint computation happens in your browser and stays there.
  • What this page stored
    Nothing
    No cookies. No localStorage. No sessionStorage. No IndexedDB. When you close this tab, this page forgets you exist.
